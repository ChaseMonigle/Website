Fraternity

During my freshman year, I decided to take a chance and become a Founding Father of the Theta Xi chapter at Florida Tech, starting with only eight members. It was an opportunity filled with challenges, but also one that pushed me to grow as a leader and teammate.

Over time, I helped expand the chapter into a 40+ member fraternity. Through this growth, I learned the importance of accountability, communication, and building a culture where every member feels valued. My involvement eventually led me to serve as Brotherhood Chair, where I worked to design events and traditions that strengthened the relationships and unity within our brotherhood.

The experience taught me organizational and leadership skills that continue to shape how I approach challenges today. This past year, our chapter reached a major milestone when we were officially chartered. As a highlight of that moment, I had the honor of being featured on the front cover of Theta Xi’s national magazine — a recognition not just of my personal efforts, but of the collective dedication of our members.

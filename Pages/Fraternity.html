Fraternity

During my freshman year, I decided to take a leap of faith and become a Founding Father of the Theta Xi chapter at Florida Tech, which began with only eight members. It was a challenge and an opportunity — one that pushed me to grow both personally and professionally.

Over time, I helped expand the fraternity into a 40+ member brotherhood. This experience taught me the value of teamwork, accountability, and persistence. As our membership grew, so did my responsibilities, eventually leading me to serve as Brotherhood Chair, where I worked to create events and traditions that strengthened our bonds and built a lasting culture of support.

The journey wasn’t always easy, but it was deeply rewarding. I developed leadership, organizational, and conflict-resolution skills that I carry into every aspect of my academic and professional life. This past year, our fraternity achieved a milestone by being officially chartered — a recognition of the hard work and dedication of every member. As an added honor, I was featured on the front cover of Theta Xi’s national magazine, symbolizing not just my contributions but the collective success of our chapter.

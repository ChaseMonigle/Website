Cybersecurity

My interest in cybersecurity comes from a mix of curiosity and practicality — I enjoy exploring how systems really work, and how to keep them secure and usable at the same time. I’ve worked with tools like FTK Imager for forensic disk imaging and verification, and Wireshark for analyzing network traffic. These tools have helped me build confidence in both digital forensics and network security.

At Florida Tech, I’m part of FITSEC, our campus cybersecurity team. We compete in Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions and hold workshops to practice challenges ranging from forensics and web exploitation to OSINT and log analysis. I also served as Team Captain for the National Cyber League (Spring 2025), where I helped organize practice sessions and guide teammates through solving challenges.

To strengthen my foundation, I completed the Google Cybersecurity Certificate, which covered security fundamentals, incident response, SIEM usage, and core networking concepts. Combined with hands-on team competitions, these experiences have given me both technical depth and leadership experience in the field.

Cybersecurity

My interest in cybersecurity comes from both curiosity and a strong desire to make systems safer and more reliable. I enjoy exploring how technologies work under the hood and applying security practices that make them easier and safer for people to use. Along the way, I’ve gained hands-on experience with tools such as FTK Imager for forensic disk imaging and verification, and Wireshark for network traffic analysis and protocol inspection.

At Florida Tech, I am an active member of FITSEC, our cybersecurity club that regularly competes in Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions. As a Team Captain for the National Cyber League (Spring 2025), I organized practice sessions, delegated challenges, and helped newer members ramp up by writing clear notes and walkthroughs. These experiences have helped me grow as both a technical problem-solver and a leader.

To strengthen my foundation, I completed the Google Cybersecurity Certificate, covering security fundamentals, incident response, SIEM usage, and core networking concepts. Looking ahead, I am interested in obtaining security clearance so that I can pursue opportunities in the federal or defense sector and work my way up in the field of security.
